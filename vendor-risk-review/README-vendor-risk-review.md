# Vendor Risk Review

This artifact is part of a GRC portfolio project based on a realistic SaaS company running in AWS and handling customer data.

I created this vendor risk review to practice evaluating third-party risk, which is a critical part of GRC work. In most environments, vendors have access to systems, data, or critical services, which introduces additional risk outside of direct organizational control.

The scenario reflects common cloud realities: identity providers, analytics platforms, customer support systems, and backup providers.

Each row represents:
- the vendor and service provided
- the level of access the vendor has to systems or data
- how critical the vendor is to business operations
- key risk areas introduced by the vendor
- a summary of the assessment based on available information
- identified control gaps
- the overall risk rating
- recommended actions to reduce risk

I also included ownership, review frequency, status, and control references to show how vendor risk is tracked over time and aligned to frameworks like SOC 2, NIST, and CIS.

This project helped me practice thinking through third-party risk in a structured way and connecting vendor dependencies to business impact and governance decisions.
