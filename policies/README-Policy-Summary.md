# Policy Update Summary

These policy files were revised for portfolio use around a realistic mid-size SaaS company operating in AWS and handling customer data.

## Changes applied across all four policies
- Removed Starks Technology Services branding and company-specific references.
- Reframed the policies around a neutral SaaS / AWS scenario.
- Shifted the framework lens away from NIST 800-171, CMMC, and DFARS-heavy wording.
- Aligned the documents to a broader portfolio-friendly governance lens using SOC 2, NIST CSF 2.0, and CIS Controls v8.
- Reduced operational boilerplate so each document reads more like a clean governance policy sample.
- Standardized layout, ownership, review cycle, and tone across the policy set.

## Policy-specific updates

### 1) Acceptable Use and Asset Management Policy
- Kept the combined acceptable use and asset management structure.
- Tightened sections around acceptable use, credential protection, BYOD, inventory, lifecycle management, and lost device reporting.
- Removed training/lab-heavy internal language that made the original version feel too organization-specific.

### 2) Incident Response Policy
- Simplified language around incident handling phases and severity.
- Reduced CUI / DFARS-specific emphasis and made the policy fit a general SaaS environment.
- Kept strong sections on reporting, evidence, communication, recovery, and lessons learned.

### 3) Privacy, Confidentiality, and Customer Data Handling Policy
- Refocused the document on customer data and confidential business information in a SaaS environment.
- Reduced the original mix of training-business and highly specialized compliance language.
- Kept practical governance areas: ownership, access, sharing, retention, disposal, and incident reporting.

### 4) Vendor and Third-Party Security Policy
- Reworked the policy around a cleaner vendor risk lifecycle: inventory, due diligence, contracts, access, monitoring, incident coordination, and termination.
- Removed STS-specific subcontractor language and compliance-heavy phrasing.
- Made the tone fit a portfolio sample for third-party risk management.

## Output note
These are portfolio-ready sample policies. They are intentionally cleaner and shorter than a fully operational enterprise policy library.
