# GRC Risk Register – AWS SaaS Scenario

## Overview
This artifact is a sample risk register for a mid-size SaaS company operating in AWS and handling customer data. It is designed to demonstrate practical GRC thinking by translating technical conditions into business risk, ownership, treatment, and control references.

## Scenario
A mid-size SaaS company hosts production workloads in AWS and stores or processes customer data. The environment relies on AWS IAM, cloud infrastructure, logging and monitoring, third-party vendors, and backups to support secure and available service delivery.

## Objective
Assess risk exposure across key security and operational areas, prioritize risks by likelihood and impact, and map each risk to recognizable control frameworks commonly used in audits and governance reviews.

## Scope
- AWS IAM
- Production cloud environment
- Logging and monitoring
- User provisioning and access reviews
- Third-party vendors
- Backups and recovery

## What is included
The spreadsheet contains:
- Risk ID
- Asset / Process
- Risk Statement
- Likelihood
- Impact
- Score
- Owner
- Treatment
- Status
- Control Reference

## Scoring approach
- Likelihood: 1–5
- Impact: 1–5
- Score: Likelihood × Impact

Higher scores indicate higher priority for treatment and oversight.

## Control mapping used
Each risk is mapped to one or more high-level control references from:
- NIST CSF 2.0
- CIS Controls v8
- SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria

These references are included to show how the risk can connect to broader compliance and control conversations.

## Notes
- This is a portfolio artifact and uses a fictional scenario.
- No real company data, systems, vendors, or customer records are included.
- The register is intended to support related artifacts such as a control assessment, vendor risk review, and SOC 2 gap analysis.
